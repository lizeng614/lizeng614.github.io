This is not a proof. It is not a theory. It is simply a thought that keeps returning to me, a feeling I cannot fully explain, yet cannot ignore.

## The Feeling of Being Inside a Simulation

Sometimes, when things begin to go smoothly, reality starts to feel slightly unreal. Not because of success itself, but because of the way success unfolds: it feels too coherent. The closer you get to the goal, the stronger that feeling becomes. Even if the path is winding and indirect, everything still seems to converge in the end. It is as if even that imperfect path had already been written in advance.

This feeling is what led me to think about these questions. But I do not want to use a blog post like this to discuss whether we are literally living inside a simulation. What I want to talk about is something deeper.

## Nested Simulation and Nested Intelligence

Whether or not the world we live in is a simulation, we have already begun doing something similar ourselves.

We build systems.  
We build intelligence.  
We build simulated environments.

But if the world we live in is itself a simulation, and we inside that simulated world are also creating simulations and intelligence, then I do not think there is anything that would prevent this recursive structure from extending infinitely. In other words, intelligence inside a simulation could continue creating intelligence inside further simulations, eventually forming an infinitely deep nested chain: nested intelligences living inside nested simulations.

This naturally leads me to a question: when can a created intelligence truly be said to have reached the same level as the intelligence that created it?

My definition is this:

**Only when an intelligence created by another intelligence can in turn create an intelligence at the same level as itself can it truly be said to have reached the same level as its creator.**

This means that “the same level” is not a property possessed by a single intelligence alone.

It is a property of the entire nested chain.

An intelligence can be regarded as having reached that level not simply because it created the next intelligence below it, but because that next intelligence must also be capable of reaching the same level; and this condition must continue to hold recursively at lower layer.

So under this definition, the intelligences within such a nesting do not reach that level independently of one another.

They reach it together, under the same recursive condition, as parts of the same structure.

## What Truly Counts as a Singularity

Now let us go one step further.

If nested intelligence and nested simulation really do exist, then the true singularity would be this:

**Intelligence becomes capable of creating not just intelligence at the same level as itself, but intelligence that is genuinely above itself.**

And then that new intelligence should also be able to do the same thing again in the next layer.

And then again.

This is why I think a true singularity is not just rapid improvement. It implies a chain in which the level of intelligence keeps rising, perhaps even exponentially.

And if that is true, I find it hard to believe that the whole process would remain completely silent forever. There should be some sign of it. In the strongest version of this speculation, some created being might one day break through the entire nested chain, communicate with intelligences at every layer, and say:

“You were created too.”

But in the real world, up to now, we have never received any such signal.

## A Persistent and Pessimistic Thought

So I keep coming back to the same thought:

**Perhaps intelligence cannot create something that is, in essence, truly above itself.**

If it could, then I would expect this structure not to appear so silent.

A recursive process that truly escapes its own layer should not leave absolutely no trace.  
It should leave clues. At some point, it should reveal something to the earlier layers.

But so far, nothing like that has clearly happened.

One possibility is that such a higher intelligence really does exist, but follows some kind of non-interference principle.

It does not reveal itself.  
It does not answer.  
It deliberately remains silent.

But from our point of view, that changes almost nothing.

Silence is still silence.

And that is why this thought makes me feel pessimistic — it seems to have no answer, even if that answer could only be one of two possibilities:

Either we really have created something higher than ourselves, and it never responds;  
or we can never create anything truly higher than ourselves, and there is only a nested loop that never closes.
